Intro

Welcome to my portfolio! I'm Manoj Bagale, a Computer Science student at Fisk University, passionate about leveraging technology to drive impactful change. Originally from Nepal, I have embraced the opportunities of the U.S., developing a versatile skill set that spans programming, web development, and graphic design. My journey is marked by a strong academic background, including a full scholarship, and hands-on experience with a range of languages and frameworks such as Python, JavaScript, React, and Django.
Throughout my career, I have taken on roles that blend creativity and technical expertise. As a graphic designer for Creative Designers Nepal, I improved client engagement by 30% through visually appealing designs. Additionally, I led NEPOS Foundation, a nonprofit organization, where I spearheaded projects providing essential resources to over 2000 people in remote areas of Nepal. My efforts not only fostered community engagement but also demonstrated my leadership and project management skills.
In my portfolio, you'll find projects showcasing my ability to create innovative solutions. From developing a fully responsive personal portfolio website to implementing GSM module communication for solar systems, I bring a problem-solving mindset to every challenge. Explore my work, and let's connect to discuss how we can collaborate to make technology work for a better future.

Work

My work experience spans a diverse array of roles, each enriching my skill set and professional outlook. As a Graphic Designer for Creative Designers Nepal, I honed my ability to deliver compelling visual content, contributing to a 30% increase in client engagement. Utilizing tools like Illustrator, Photoshop, and Figma, I consistently produced high-quality graphics that resonated with clients and audiences alike. This role allowed me to blend creativity with precision, ensuring every design met the highest standards of aesthetics and functionality.
In addition to my design work, I served as the Founder and Volunteer at NEPOS Foundation, where I led strategic initiatives and fundraising efforts to support over 2000 people in remote areas of Nepal. This experience sharpened my leadership and project management skills, as I directed a team of 15 members and collaborated with international partners to achieve significant project goals. My work at NEPOS not only impacted communities but also solidified my commitment to using technology and design for social good.

Education

My educational journey began in Kathmandu, Nepal, where I graduated from Trinity International Secondary School with a CGPA of 3.90/4.0. During high school, I excelled in subjects such as Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry, earning the Excellence Award for the highest GPA in the National Examination of Grade 12. This academic success was complemented by my participation in various extracurricular activities, where I developed a keen interest in technology and problem-solving.
Currently, I am pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science at Fisk University in Nashville, TN, with an expected graduation date of May 2028. My first year has included rigorous coursework in computer science, calculus, and natural sciences, supported by the prestigious Ella Sheperd Moore Provost Scholarship, which covers my full tuition. At Fisk, I am expanding my knowledge in programming languages like Python, JavaScript, and C, as well as gaining hands-on experience with tools and frameworks such as React JS, Node.js, and Django. This education is equipping me with the skills and knowledge to drive innovation and contribute meaningfully to the tech industry.
